The Evolution of Operating Systems

The evolution of operating systems began in the 1950s with simple batch processing systems. Over time, OS technology advanced to include multitasking, graphical interfaces, and complex memory management. Today, operating systems power everything — from mobile devices to supercomputers.

Key Innovator

Gary Kildall

Creator of CP/M Operating System

Gary Kildall was a pioneering computer scientist who created CP/M (Control Program for Microcomputers), one of the first widely used operating systems for personal computers. His work laid the foundation for modern PC operating systems.
